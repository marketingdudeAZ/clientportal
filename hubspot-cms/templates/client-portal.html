How the Health Score works

Computed nightly from leasing operations data. Three inputs — physical occupancy %, ATR % (available-to-rent; lower is better), and exposure (leases expiring in the next 120 days as a % of units). Thresholds differ for stabilized vs. lease-up properties.

InputStabilizedLease-Up
Occupancy %≥95→100 · ≥93→85 · ≥90→70 · ≥87→55 · else 35≥88→90 · ≥75→75 · ≥60→60 · ≥45→45 · else 30
ATR % (lower better)≤4→100 · ≤6→80 · ≤9→60 · ≤13→40 · else 20≤10→90 · ≤20→75 · ≤35→55 · ≤50→35 · else 20
Exposure (120d exp ÷ units)≤8%→100 · ≤15%→75 · ≤22%→50 · else 25not used
WeightingOcc 50% · ATR 30% · Exposure 20%Occ 60% · ATR 40%
≥75 ON TRACK 50–74 WATCH <50 NEEDS ATTENTION

+ Add / edit a context note for this property →

These bands also drive the dashboard RED/YLW/GRN mix and the “What needs you today” severity. The fuller 4-category Red Light score (marketing / market / funnel / experience) is being re-enabled as its data feed is rebuilt.

Property targets
A lease-up is scored against a linear ramp from the takeover date to the target over this many months — being behind pace flags it, ahead of pace scores higher.
